We see it all the time. A homeowner spends all weekend cutting down overgrown honeysuckle and dead limbs. They feel great—until they look at the **massive pile of brush** in the driveway.

Now what?

In **Joplin**, getting rid of brush can be a headache. Here are your options.

## 1. Burning (The Risky Option)
*   **The Law:** Burning inside Joplin city limits is strictly regulated. You usually need a burn permit, and you can only burn natural vegetation (no trash) during specific hours.
*   **The Risk:** Missouri winds are unpredictable. A small brush fire can quickly turn into a structure fire. 

## 2. Hauling to the Dump
*   **The Cost:** You need a truck and a trailer. You have to load it, drive to the compost facility, pay the gate fee, and unload it manually.
*   **The Effort:** It usually takes 4-5 trips for a medium-sized brush pile.

## 3. Professional Chipping (The Easy Way)
This is what we do.
We bring a commercial wood chipper to your driveway.
*   **Speed:** Our machine eats 12-inch logs instantly.
*   **Volume Reduction:** A mountain of brush the size of a car turns into a small pile of wood chips.
*   **Zero Effort:** We drag the brush. We feed the machine. You drink coffee.

## The "Mulch" Bonus
Once we chip your brush, you have a choice:
1.  **Haul Away:** We blast the chips into our truck and take them away. Your yard is clean.
2.  **Keep the Chips:** We blast the chips into a pile for you. You now have free, native mulch for your flower beds or garden paths.
