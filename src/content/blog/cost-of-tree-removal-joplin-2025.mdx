"What's the damage?"
We hear this every day. Whether you have a dead Ash tree or a massive Walnut that needs to go, price is always the top concern.

In **Joplin**, tree work pricing depends on access, danger, and disposal. Here is the honest breakdown of what you can expect to pay in 2025.

## Average Pricing in Joplin

### 1. Small Trees (Under 30ft)
*   **Cost:** **$250 – $550**
*   **Examples:** Redbuds, Dogwoods, small Fruit Trees.
*   **Time:** 1-2 hours.

### 2. Medium Trees (30-60ft)
*   **Cost:** **$550 – $1,000**
*   **Examples:** Bradford Pears, Maples, Hackberries.
*   **Note:** This price goes up if the tree is over a fence or house.

### 3. Large Trees (60ft+)
*   **Cost:** **$1,000 – $2,500+**
*   **Examples:** Mature Pin Oaks, Sycamores, Walnuts.
*   **Complexity:** These usually require a bucket truck or climber.

### 4. Hazardous / Crane Removals
*   **Cost:** **$2,500 – $5,000+**
*   **Scenario:** A massive tree that is hollow, dead, and leaning over your master bedroom. We cannot climb it (too dangerous), so we must bring in a crane to lift it out piece by piece.

## Factors That Increase Price
1.  **Power Lines:** If we have to work around Liberty Utilities lines, it slows us down.
2.  **Backyard Access:** Can we get our truck to the tree? If we have to drag brush by hand through a narrow gate, labor costs go up.
3.  **The "Rocky Soil" Factor:** Joplin has notoriously rocky soil. Stump grinding here destroys our machine teeth faster than in other cities. Expect slightly higher stump grinding fees ($4-$7 per inch) due to the rocks.

## The "Walnut" Bonus
Joplin has amazing **Black Walnut** trees.
*   **The Myth:** "You will pay ME for the wood, right?"
*   **The Reality:** Unless you have a logging truck's worth of veneer-quality logs (straight, no knots for 12 feet), the wood is not worth enough to offset the removal cost. However, we *can* sometimes lower the price if the logs are easily accessible and high quality.

## Cheap vs. Safe
In Joplin, you can find a guy with a pickup truck who will do it for half our price.
**But ask yourself:**
*   Does he have insurance? (If he drops the tree on your house, YOU pay).
*   Does he have Workers' Comp? (If he falls, YOU get sued).
