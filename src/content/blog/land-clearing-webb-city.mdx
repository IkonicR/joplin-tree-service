The suburbs of Joplin—**Webb City**, **Carl Junction**, and **Oronogo**—are growing fast. We see new construction everywhere.

If you just bought a few acres of wooded land to build your dream home, you have a big job ahead of you: **Land Clearing.**

It’s not as simple as just bulldozing everything flat. Here is how to clear your lot intelligently.

## 1. Mark the "Keepers" First
Once a tree is gone, it takes 50 years to replace it. Before any heavy machinery shows up:
*   **Walk the lot** with a can of orange spray paint.
*   **Mark the trees you want to save.** Look for healthy Oaks, straight Walnuts, and mature Maples.
*   **Clear around them.** We can use forestry mulchers to clear the underbrush while leaving your prize trees untouched.

## 2. Forestry Mulching vs. Bulldozing
For underbrush (briars, small cedars, poison ivy), **Forestry Mulching** is superior to bulldozing.
*   **No Burn Piles:** The machine shreds the vegetation into a layer of mulch on the forest floor.
*   **Erosion Control:** The mulch layer prevents rain from washing your topsoil away (which is a huge issue in Jasper County).
*   **Nutrients:** The decomposing mulch feeds the remaining trees.

## 3. Clearing the Foundation Pad
For the actual footprint of your house and driveway, we need to go deeper.
*   **Stump Removal:** You cannot build a foundation on top of tree stumps. They will rot and cause the slab to crack. We must dig out the root balls completely in the building zone.
*   **Grading:** We rough grade the area to ensure water flows away from your future homesite.

## 4. Dealing with Cedars
Eastern Red Cedars are invasive in Missouri. They drink a ton of water and choke out grass.
*   **The Strategy:** We usually recommend clearing almost all Cedars unless they are providing a specific privacy screen. They are fire hazards and harbor bagworms.

## 5. Permits
If you are disturbing more than 1 acre of land, you may need a land disturbance permit from the **Missouri DNR** or the local municipality to ensure silt doesn't run off into creeks.
